Customer Risk Prioritisation Redesign

Revealed complex ownership structures across companies and directors to enable accurate remediation and reduce regulatory risk in a banking environment.

The Problem

In a regulated banking environment, the organisation needed to remediate customer records by updating contact details for companies and their owners to meet compliance requirements.

However, there was no clear visibility of how entities were connected. Companies, directors, trustees and third parties were treated as largely independent, and ownership structures were assumed to be simple and hierarchical. In reality, a single director could be linked to multiple companies across different structures.

This created a high risk of missing key individuals during remediation, incomplete or incorrect updates, audit failure and potential regulatory fines.

What I Did

I transformed the problem from a fragmented, table-based view into a network representation of relationships.

I built a graphical map of entities and relationships, including companies, directors, trustees and third-party links. I modelled the connections using a node-and-edge structure to reflect real-world ownership and control relationships.

This exposed how entities were interconnected across multiple layers and gave stakeholders a clear visual view of the full structure for the first time.

Key Insight

What appeared to be a set of simple, independent entities was actually a highly interconnected network of relationships.

Complex and non-obvious ownership links were uncovered, including directors connected to multiple companies and cross-entity dependencies spanning different ownership layers.

This enabled accurate identification of who needed to be contacted for remediation and removed blind spots from the process.

Impact

The analysis enabled a targeted and structured remediation strategy. It helped ensure no key entities or individuals were missed, improved contact prioritisation and reduced the risk of regulatory fines and audit failure.

It also significantly improved efficiency compared with the previous ad-hoc approach.

Stakeholder Outcome

Business stakeholders responded very positively because the analysis gave them visibility they did not previously have. The work became a core input into remediation planning and led to further requests for deeper analysis.
