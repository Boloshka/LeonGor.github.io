Critical Data Lineage for Teller System Migration

Enabled safe migration of a legacy teller system by uncovering hidden data dependencies and reconstructing data flows across the bank.

The Problem

National Australia Bank relied on a legacy teller system (eBOBS) that had been in operation for decades.

Transaction data flowed through a mainframe process into the Global Data Warehouse, supporting critical reporting across compliance, financial crime and branch operations.

Over time, many undocumented users and dependencies had built up around this data, with limited visibility into how it was used.

When the bank decided to replace the system with a modern microservices platform, this lack of clarity created a major risk: critical dashboards and reporting processes could break during migration.

What I Did

I established visibility of how data flowed from the legacy system into the warehouse and how it was used across the organisation.

  • Identified and engaged previously unknown downstream users
  • Reverse-engineered data flows using SQL and system tracing
  • Mapped key fields and their usage across reporting and operational processes
  • Defined the data requirements needed for the new system to replicate legacy outputs

Key Insight

The biggest risk was not the migration itself, but hidden dependencies across the organisation.

Many critical processes relied on specific fields and transformations that were not documented. Without uncovering these dependencies, the migration could silently break important reporting.

Impact

  • Enabled a safe and controlled migration approach
  • Ensured continuity of critical dashboards and reporting
  • Reduced risk of compliance and operational issues
  • Provided clarity for engineering and business teams to proceed with confidence

Stakeholder Outcome

  • Gave stakeholders visibility into previously unclear data usage
  • Supported coordination between business, engineering and platform teams
  • Became a key input into migration planning
