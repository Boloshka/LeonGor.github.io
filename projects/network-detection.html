Proactive Network Issue Detection

Detected broadband service issues before customers complained, enabling earlier intervention and improved customer experience.

The Problem

Broadband network issues were often identified only after large numbers of customers started contacting support. By that point, the issue had already affected customer experience, increased complaint volumes, damaged brand perception and created churn risk.

The existing approach was largely reactive: wait for complaints, investigate the issue, then respond. This meant delays, repeated customer frustration and unnecessary pressure on technical and customer-facing teams.

What I Did

I proposed a proactive detection approach using a combination of customer modem telemetry and network-level deep packet inspection metrics.

The aim was to identify early signals of broadband service degradation before customers raised complaints. These insights could then be passed to technical teams for investigation and to customer-facing teams so they could proactively inform or support affected customers.

Key Insight

By the time customers complain, the service problem has already become visible and frustrating.

However, many broadband issues leave early warning signals in modem and network telemetry. By detecting these patterns earlier, the organisation could shift from reactive complaint handling to proactive service management.

Impact

The approach significantly reduced complaint rates by allowing a large portion of service issues to be detected and addressed proactively.

The work was tested with customer-facing teams, received strong management support and was selected for productionisation.

It helped improve customer experience, protect brand perception and reduce churn risk by resolving or communicating issues before they escalated.
